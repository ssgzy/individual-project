From Baseline to Better Results: Notes from a Text Classification Project

2026-02-16 · NLP

The first step was to stop relying on “single run wins.” In team assignments, text-classification performance can look unstable if preprocessing, sampling, and reporting are not standardized.

In this project, we first compared baseline settings, then isolated improvements one by one: cleaning strategy, vectorization choice, training settings, and post-processing. Each change was run with fixed seeds and logged with clear notes.

The most meaningful gains came from reproducibility structure itself. Once the process was comparable across runs, tuning became less noisy and findings became easier to defend in review.

My takeaway is simple: good results come from repeatable experiments, not only model selection. A strong process often improves both short-term score and long-term maintainability.
